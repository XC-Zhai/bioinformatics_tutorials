Aylward lab Bioinformatics tutorials and SOPs


These are a series of introductory bionformatics tutorials I have developed, primarily for teaching Systems Biology classes at Virginia Tech and also for archiving smaller protocols used in my lab group.

The "chapters" or tutorials below are intended for use at the introductory level (i.e., starting with little or no bioinformatics knowledge). They presume a Unix or Unix-like environment (primarily Ubuntu or CentOS).

Main chapters
Ch. 1: Introduction to basic Unix commands

Introduction to the Unix environment - useful commands - navigating directories

Ch. 2: Introduction to parsing FASTA files

Introduction to DNA and protein sequences in FASTA format - parsing files with seqkit

Ch. 2: Introduction to Gene Prediction

Downloading genomes from NCBI - Predicting protein-coding genes with Prodigal - predicting rRNA genes with barrnap

Ch. 2: Introduction to Homology Searches with BLAST

Introduction to Hidden Markov Models - multi-sequence alignments of proteins - working with hmmsearch
